What is an EPO charge?

Plain-English context, so you know exactly what this is.

An Early Payoff (EPO) occurs when a loan is paid off or refinanced within a short window after closing — usually inside the first 4 to 6 months. When that happens, the investor recaptures the premium they paid us at funding because they didn’t earn the interest they expected.

Because your commission was paid out of that same premium at closing, the company is required to recover a portion of it when the premium is clawed back. This is industry-standard practice across every lender — it is not a penalty, and it does not reflect on your performance.

The amount shown above is strictly your share of the chargeback on this specific loan. No other deductions, overhead, or unrelated costs are included.

One more thing you should know: before this notice reached you, we went back to the lender and pushed for every concession available, leaning on long-standing relationships and our track record wherever we could. The figure above reflects the lowest number we were able to negotiate. It is not a first-ask amount; it is the best outcome we could achieve on your behalf.
